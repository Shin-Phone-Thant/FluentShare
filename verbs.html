What are Verbs?




A verb is a word that expresses an action, occurrence, or state of being. Verbs are one of the essential parts of a sentence, as they describe what the subject does or is.

Some examples:


Action Verbs Examples:run, eat, think, play, write
Example Sentences:
She runs every morning.
I think about my future often.
Linking Verbs Examples:is, are, was, were, become, seem
Example Sentences:
He is a doctor.
The cake looks delicious.
Helping Verbs (Auxiliary Verbs) Examples:is, have, has, had, will, shall, can, could, may, might
Example Sentences:
He is reading a book.
She will go to the park tomorrow.
Transitive Verbs Examples: kick, throw, write
Example Sentences:
He kicked the ball.
She wrote a letter.
Intransitive Verbs Examples: sleep, cry, run
Example Sentences:
The baby cried loudly
She ran to the park.
Verb Forms: Base Form: play, go, eat
Past Form: played, went, ate
Past Participle: played, gone, eaten
Past Participle: playing, going, eating

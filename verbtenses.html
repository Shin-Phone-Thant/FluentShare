What are verb tenses?




Verb tenses are grammatical forms that indicate when an action or state occurs in relation to the present, past, or future. They help convey the timing of an event, and they are essential for understanding when something happens, is happening, or will happen. In English, verb tenses can be classified into three main categories based on time: past, present, and future. Each of these categories can be broken down further into different aspects that show the state of the action (whether it's completed, ongoing, habitual, etc.).




Summary of Basic Tenses:

Present Tense

- Simple Present: Describes actions that are habitual, general truths, or facts
  • Example: She reads every morning.
- Present Continuous (Progressive): Describes actions happening right now or around the present moment.
  • Example: She is reading a book.
- Present Perfect: Describes actions that started in the past but are relevant to the present.
  • Example: She has read five books this month.
- Present Perfect Continuous: Describes actions that began in the past and are still continuing or were happening until recently.
  • Example: She has been reading for two hours.

Past Tense

- Simple Past: Describes actions that happened at a specific time in the past
  • Example: She read the book yesterday.

- Past Continuous (Progressive): Describes actions that were ongoing at a specific time in the past.
  • Example: She was reading when I called her.

- Past Perfect: Describes actions that were completed before another action in the past.
  • Example: She had already read the book when I gave it to her.

- Past Perfect Continuous: Describes actions that were ongoing in the past and were completed before another event.
  • Example: She had been reading for two hours when I called.

Future Tense

- Simple Future: Describes actions that will happen at a later time.
  • Example: She will read the book tomorrow.

- Future Continuous (Progressive): Describes actions that will be ongoing at a specific time in the future.
  • Example: She will be reading at 8 p.m. tomorrow.

- Future Perfect: Describes actions that will be completed before a certain point in the future.
  • Example: She will have read the book by tomorrow.

- Future Perfect Continuous: Describes actions that will be ongoing in the future and will continue until a specific point.
  • Example: She will have been reading for two hours by the time I call her.
